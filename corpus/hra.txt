Lecture 4
	•	Three waves of people analytics technology
	•	First wave: core HR
	•	Companies moved from tradition HR software where they hosted their own servers to SaaS-based HR platforms that operate in the cloud 
	•	Can benefit from regular updates and improvements without managing the software infrastructure themselves 
	•	While cloud-based HR systems provide a strong foundation, they aren’t enough for deep analysis 
	•	Data models: system lack well-structure data models making it hard to analyze HR data effectively
	•	Incomplete story: HR professional struggle to access and integrate data from multiple sources so they only see a part of the picture, limiting their ability to make informed decisions 
	•	Predictive analytics: lack of predictive analysis meant they had limited insights and no ability to prescribe recommendations for action
	•	Data inclusion: unable to add specialist data from emerging technology platforms so complex business topics are less likely to be undertaken using HR core alone
	•	They can’t deliver analytics beyond reporting, dashboard and scorecards and even then, are limited to data within their own system
	•	Second wave: analytics dashboards
	•	Before, executives didn’t understand or care about effort, cost or technology behind making report as long as they were available. The report was created on demand and on-premise
	•	Now companies still use people analytics only when needed and HR teams struggle with inefficient, time-consuming manual reporting 
	•	Companies needed dashboards that combine data from multiple HR and business systems. However, this leads to a lot of challenges.
	•	Cost: companies see these platforms as an extra expense even though they are cheaper than core HR systems 
	•	Perceived value: businesses underestimate the true impact of analytics on performance, focusing only on HR benefits and not business outcomes. They focus on soft benefits (time) rather than hard benefits
	•	HR capability: Many HR professionals lack technical skills, limiting their ability to communicate effectively with stakeholders and drive data-driven decision-making
	•	Benefits of implementing people analytics dashboard successfully
	•	Executive insights: C-suite executives receive consistent, accurate information about their workforce in real time
	•	Democratization of data: the opportunity to democratize data to all managers in the business improves decision making around people and teams 
	•	Speed of decision making: HR can provide insights instantly instead of waiting for analytics to compile reports 
	•	Improved credibility of HR: HR gains trust and influence by providing accurate, real-time data across all systems 
	•	Cost savings: reducing time spent on manual data consolidation leads to significant cost savings 
	•	Third wave: specialist people analytics technology
	•	New technologies focus on workforce panning, talent and market intelligence, behavioral skills, productivity etc. 
	•	technology is transforming people analytics, with specialized tools providing deeper insights into workforce behavior and performance.
	•	Cloud-based solutions are modernizing traditional HR functions, making them more data-driven and efficient
	•	Used well they can excite business leaders to take action but if they’re used poorly, they can be an expensive mistake. Before investing:
	•	Start with the business question and understand what the problem is. Third wave tools help provide new insights.
	•	Use the right combination of technologies: some third wave providers have products that are highly specialist in nature however a solving analytical questions require more tools than a single multi-elements core HR system. Might need a combination of technologies.
	•	Be mindful of privacy and ethics:  Third Wave technologies gather and analyze data that can be considered invasive so whether to use the solutions is a question for legal teams.
	•	Before buying people analytics technology consider
	•	Indicators that buying technology is needed
	•	Lack of existing solutions: available tech won't help analyze a clearly defined and highly prioritized business problem 
	•	Tech experts are not available 
	•	Limited time to build the right solution internally due to urgency of problem
	•	How to navigate the vendor market
	•	Keep up with market trends: stay informed about latest HR tech and assign a team member to routinely research vendor and update team
	•	Build a network of industry peers: connect with peers who already work with a specific HR tech company which helps you understand the pros and cons of each vendor
	•	Have a shortlist of companies and list of vendors by category to save time when businesses suddenly require a solution and enhance HR’s credibility
	•	Working with procurement specialists
	•	Procurement teams help navigate vendor selection and costs
	•	Treat procurement colleagues well: don’t wait till you urgently need a tool, establish trust beforehand. A strong partnership ensures that HR gets the best tech at the best price
	•	Respect their expertise: they understand the market and risks to instead of dictating choices, collaborate and let them guide the selection process
	•	Help procurement understand the HR business problem: explain why new analytics tool is needed and how it will benefit the company.
	•	Ten key question to consider when buying technology
	•	Does the product address the needs of all countries in scope
	•	What are the hidden costs
	•	What is the approach to privacy 
	•	What is required of the people analytics team, both time and resource?
	•	What is the policy for access to raw data that is gathered by the vendor?
	•	What are the implementation stages for conducting a pilot and for implementing across the enterprise?
	•	How quickly is the technology integrated into core systems?
	•	What failures have occurred with the product/solution and what was learnt?
	•	What customer and technical support is provided?
	•	What is the vendor’s financial stability (if a small firm)?
	•	What to look for in a vendor partnership
	•	Real partnership: includes influence on their product road map, levels of support during implementation, and whether vendor will make data available at no extra cost
	•	Conducting pilots: instead of running full scale they can implement a pilot/ trial run to assess if the tool works well before making the financial commitment 
	•	Privacy: understand the vendors approach to data security and privacy
